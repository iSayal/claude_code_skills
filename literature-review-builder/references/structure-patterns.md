# Literature Review Structure Patterns

## Overview

Literature reviews can be organized in multiple ways. The choice depends on research aims, amount of existing literature, and the story you want to tell.

---

## Thematic Structure (Recommended)

**Best for:** Extensive literature, complex topics, identifying patterns and debates

### Pattern

```
Introduction
├── Define scope & research question
├── Preview themes
└── Identify research gap

Theme 1: [Concept/Debate/Idea]
├── Overview of theme
├── Synthesize sources supporting view A
├── Synthesize sources supporting view B
├── Highlight tensions/contradictions
└── Connect to overall research question

Theme 2: [Concept/Debate/Idea]
├── Overview of theme
├── Synthesize findings across sources
├── Methodological differences affecting results
└── How this theme relates to Theme 1

Theme 3: [Concept/Debate/Idea]
├── Overview of theme
├── Synthesize findings
├── Emergent patterns or surprises
└── Connection to broader themes

[Additional themes as needed]

Conclusion
├── Synthesize across all themes
├── Consensus vs. disagreement
├── Research gaps identified
└── Directions for future research
```

### When to Use

- Large body of literature (>15-20 sources)
- Multiple perspectives or debates exist
- Goal is identifying patterns and themes
- Social sciences, business, education

### Key Principle

Group by **ideas**, not by authors or time. Show how sources relate to each other.

## Chronological Structure

**Best for:** Limited studies, historical topics, showing evolution of ideas

### Pattern

```
Introduction
└── Define scope & timeline

Early Period (e.g., 2000-2010)
├── Dominant theories & methods
├── Key findings
└── Limitations

Middle Period (e.g., 2011-2019)
├── Shifts in theory or method
├── New findings building on early work
└── Emerging debates

Recent Period (e.g., 2020-2025)
├── Current state of research
├── Latest developments
└── Unresolved questions

Conclusion
├── Trajectory of research over time
└── How historical context informs current understanding
```

### When to Use

- Limited number of sources (<15)
- Clear historical progression
- Showing how understanding evolved
- Historical analysis, technology evolution

### Key Principle

Trace the **development** of ideas, not just list sources by date.

---

## Methodological Structure

**Best for:** Comparing research approaches, evaluating methods

### Pattern

```
Introduction
└── Research question & importance of methodology

Method A (e.g., Quantitative Studies)
├── Overview of approach
├── Key findings from this method
├── Strengths & limitations
└── How method shapes conclusions

Method B (e.g., Qualitative Studies)
├── Overview of approach
├── Key findings
├── Strengths & limitations
└── Comparison with Method A findings

Method C (e.g., Mixed Methods)
├── Overview
├── Key findings
└── Integration of A and B

Conclusion
├── How methodology affects findings
├── Best methods for this research question
└── Methodological gaps
```

### When to Use

- Research methods significantly affect findings
- Comparing different approaches
- Evaluating quality of evidence
- Evidence-based practice fields

---

## Funnel Structure (For Focused Reviews)

**Best for:** Narrow research questions, systematic reviews

### Pattern

```
Introduction
└── Specific research question

Broad Context
└── General state of field

Narrowing Focus
└── Sub-topic directly related to question

Directly Relevant Studies
├── Most relevant sources
├── Detailed analysis
└── Synthesis

Gap Analysis
└── What's missing from existing research

Implications
└── How findings answer research question
```

### When to Use

- Systematic reviews
- Meta-analyses
- Answering specific research questions
- Building argument for new research

---

## Hybrid Structures

Combine approaches based on needs:

### Thematic-Chronological
```
Theme 1
├── Early research (2000-2010)
├── Recent developments (2011-2025)
└── Current understanding

Theme 2
├── Early research
├── Recent developments
└── Current understanding
```

### Methodological-Thematic
```
Quantitative Findings
├── Theme A
├── Theme B
└── Theme C

Qualitative Findings
├── Theme A
├── Theme B
└── Theme C

Comparison
└── Where methods align/conflict
```

---

## Structure Selection Guide

| Literature Situation | Recommended Structure |
|---------------------|---------------------|
| Extensive, multiple perspectives | **Thematic** |
| Limited sources | **Chronological** |
| Methods significantly affect results | **Methodological** |
| Historical evolution is key | **Chronological** |
| Identifying patterns across fields | **Thematic** |
| Systematic review, specific question | **Funnel** |
| Comparing approaches | **Methodological** or **Hybrid** |

---

## Common Structural Elements

### Introduction Components (in order)
1. Hook or context
2. Define topic scope
3. State research question/focus
4. Preview structure (themes or approach)
5. Identify research gap (for academic work)

### Body Section Pattern
1. Topic sentence/section thesis
2. Overview of theme
3. Synthesize supporting sources
4. Acknowledge opposing/contradictory views
5. Analyze methodological differences
6. Connect to research question

### Conclusion Components
1. Restate research question and scope
2. Synthesize key findings across sections
3. Identify consensus vs. disagreement
4. Highlight research gaps
5. Implications for practice/future research
